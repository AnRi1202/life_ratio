life ratio

Make the shape of your time visible.

Enter your drift time, daily tasks, and effort time to visualize how your life has been spent so far.

There is no universal answer for what counts as wasted time. What matters, what feels worth keeping, and what you want less of all differ from person to person. life ratio is not for blaming your past. It is for making the shape of the life you want easier to see.

Starting from Step 1 is enough. You can view the results before filling in everything, then come back and refine.

Step 2

Then add the time you want less of

What feels wasteful is different for everyone. Instead of using a general rule, enter the time you personally want less of. This can include not only entertainment, but also things like draining social plans, overthinking, and procrastination. The suggestions are grouped by type to make them easier to scan.

Step 3

Add routine time if it helps

This section is not the main character. It is the baseline that helps place everything else in context. By entering things like sleep and meals, you can see how heavy your drift time and effort time really are inside your full life.

Step 4

Finish with time you built on purpose

Enter the time that feels genuinely built when you look back on it. Study, training, reading, making, qualification prep, research. This is the time you want to leave to your future self.
